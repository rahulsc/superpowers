# Greeting Conventions

This file defines shared conventions for the `hello-world` pack's greeting workflow. Apply these rules when drafting any greeting message.

## Tone Guidelines

| Audience | Tone | Example opener |
|----------|------|----------------|
| End users | Warm, casual | "Welcome! We're glad you're here." |
| Developers | Direct, informative | "Getting started: here's what you need." |
| Stakeholders | Professional, confident | "Thank you for joining us today." |

## Structure

A well-formed greeting has three parts:

1. **Opening** — acknowledge the reader directly
2. **Context** — one sentence on what they're being welcomed to
3. **Next step** — a single clear call to action or pointer

## Checklist

Before delivering a greeting, confirm:
- [ ] Audience is identified (user / developer / stakeholder)
- [ ] Tone matches the audience row in the table above
- [ ] Greeting has all three structural parts (opening, context, next step)
- [ ] No jargon used for non-developer audiences
- [ ] Length: under 50 words for UI copy, under 150 words for documentation

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid these in greetings:
- Overly formal language for casual user-facing messages ("We wish to extend our warmest salutations...")
- Missing next step (greeting without a call to action leaves readers uncertain)
- Jargon or acronyms unexplained for general audiences
- Exclamation marks used more than once in a single greeting
